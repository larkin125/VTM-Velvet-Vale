Terminology

The Silence

The inviolate law of secrecy. To break The Silence is to invite extinction — not only for the transgressor but for their entire Brood. The Silence is both necessity and faith, the foundation upon which the Concord endures.

The Blooded

The immortal inheritors of the dark gift — neither living nor dead, but sustained by Sanguis, the life-essence of the mortal world. Among mortals, they move unseen, their existence veiled by the Silence.

Regent / Marquis

Rulers of the cities of the Blooded. Each domain is governed by a Regent, though many style themselves Marquis or Marchioness to emphasize their noble heritage. Their authority flows from ancient bloodline pacts and the sanction of The Crimson Concord itself.

Elders

The ancients of the Blooded, whose power and influence shape the will of the Concord. They form The Inner Circles, advising or manipulating their Regents through centuries of scheming and subtle diplomacy.

House / Lineage / Brood

Families of the Blooded, tied by shared ancestry or ideology. A House is a political and social entity; a Lineage denotes direct descent; a Brood signifies a family of convenience — those who share a Patron but not necessarily a bond of loyalty.

Patron

The one who bestows the dark gift — mentor, progenitor, and often master. A Patron bears both honour and responsibility for their Descendants, and their actions echo through the ages.

Descendant

The newly made among the Blooded, marked forever by the essence of their Patron. Some call them “fledglings,” others “heirs,” though to their Elders they are pawns in a timeless game.

Thrall

A mortal bound by the Sanguis of a Blooded being. Neither free nor fully enslaved, a Thrall is sustained and enthralled by the taste of their master’s Sanguis. Many serve as attendants, spies, or lovers, believing their servitude a kind of grace.

Sanguis

The vital elixir that animates the Blooded — drawn from mortal veins or from other immortals in acts of sacrament or violence. It is at once nourishment, currency, and communion.
