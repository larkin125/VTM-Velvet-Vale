Session 3 Summary

🕯️ Session 3 Summary — “The Velvet Veil, Part III: The Fractured Rose”

Chronicle: Velvet Veil
Coterie: Lucian Valemont (Toreador), Evelyn Crowe (Ghoul), Adrian Vale (Ghoul)
Setting: The Velvet Veil, London — several weeks after Session 2

Act I — The Quiet Before

Lucian and his ghoul, Adrian Vale, spent long nights in the study of The Velvet Veil, researching the mysterious Rosethorn Pact. Adrian discovered that Lucian’s name was written among the founders—added hastily, on the same night of his Embrace by Lord Adrian Valemont, his sire. Lucian confessed that he had no memory of joining the Pact, realizing that his transformation and the Pact’s creation were intertwined.

Tensions rose when Adrian questioned Lucian’s humanity and the nature of the Beast. Lucian lashed out, then immediately regretted it—embracing the boy and apologizing. Their bond deepened as master and ghoul, but Lucian’s admission of what he might do to Adrian—"I will probably use you, like my sire used me"—cast a shadow over their closeness.

Act II — The Fractured Muse

After sending Adrian away in a moment of anger, Lucian destroyed his unfinished painting in a violent outburst—tearing the canvas and shattering a wine glass against it. Evelyn arrived to find the aftermath, calm and resolute as ever. Lucian, shaken by his own rage, confessed his remorse and created something new: from glass, blood, and torn canvas, he rebuilt the painting into a luminous portrait of Evelyn, a mosaic of devotion born from ruin.

He presented it to her the following morning. The refracted light made her likeness seem alive—an act of both apology and love. Evelyn, moved and uncertain, accepted the piece, sensing that it was more than art; it was confession. Their exchange reaffirmed their bond and revealed the fragile humanity that still lingered within him.

Act III — The Pact Tightens

In the quiet after, Lucian confided in Evelyn about Marianne of Bath, who had chosen death—her own ghoul staking her at her request—to escape the Pact. He feared he was walking the same path unknowingly, caught between the secret war of Prince Adrian Welles and Sir Reginald Harker. Evelyn warned him that he might not have joined the Pact by choice, but had been placed within it by design—that his Embrace and the Pact’s founding were one and the same event.

Lucian admitted his dread of being forced to kill again, hinting at a past he never spoke of. Evelyn and Adrian vowed to protect him, forming a true Coterie within The Velvet Veil. They began to plan their next moves—Evelyn to secure the house and handle politics; Adrian to investigate Grimshaw and the museum records. Together, they agreed to uncover the Pact’s origins before Harker or the Prince moved against them.

Act IV — The Artist and His Hunger

Lucian spent the following days secluded in his deepest library, recording his findings in a leather-bound journal written in his personal cipher. His body weakened with hunger until, after several sleepless nights, he finally gave in—drinking bottled blood straight from the necks, mocking his own loss of refinement.

He bathed, dressed in elegance once more, and resolved to face London anew. Restored in both spirit and resolve, he prepared to attend Elysium, intent on drawing out Harker and the Prince alike through charm and deceit. For the first time in weeks, Lucian allowed himself a flicker of satisfaction—his hunger quieted, his purpose clear.

Session Outcomes

Allies Strengthened:

  • Evelyn Crowe: reaffirmed loyalty through compassion and practical counsel.
  • Adrian Vale: elevated to true confidant and researcher within Lucian’s private circle.

Mysteries Deepened:

  • Lucian’s signature predates his memory—his Embrace and the Pact’s founding are linked.
  • Marianne’s death was deliberate, a possible attempt to break the Rosethorn’s hold.
  • The Pact’s influence appears to bind its members across centuries.

Lucian’s Standing:

  • Renewed his confidence and artistry through creation.
  • Now aware he may be a keystone within the Rosethorn’s structure.
  • Preparing to confront both Prince Welles and Sir Reginald Harker—each believing him loyal.

Themes

  • Beauty born of destruction
  • The fragility of immortality
  • Loyalty and manipulation entwined
  • Love as both salvation and curse
  • Art as confession, and truth as damnation

End of Session III — “The Fractured Rose”
